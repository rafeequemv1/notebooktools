Is NotebookLM Free? What You Get in 2026

June 23, 2026 · 8 min read

Yes, NotebookLM has a free tier for personal use with a Google account. You do not need a credit card to sign up, create notebooks, upload sources, or chat with your documents. If you are asking is NotebookLM free before diving into a research project or semester of coursework, the short answer is yes for the core experience most people need.

Google also sells upgraded access through NotebookLM Plus, often bundled with Google One AI Premium or certain Workspace plans. That paid path raises limits and may add features, but it is optional. This guide walks through what you get at no cost, when an upgrade might make sense, and how NotebookLM compares to subscription AI tools like ChatGPT Plus. For background on the product itself, see our overview of what NotebookLM is.

What is included in the free tier

The free version of NotebookLM is not a stripped-down demo. It is a full research workspace built around your own sources. Here is what most personal users get without paying anything:

  • Notebooks. Create multiple notebooks to organize projects, courses, or topics. Each notebook acts as a self-contained research hub.
  • Sources. Add PDFs, Google Docs, pasted text, web URLs, and YouTube videos. Answers are grounded in what you upload, with citations back to the original material.
  • Chat. Ask questions, request summaries, generate study outlines, and run follow-up prompts across everything in a notebook. This is the main reason people use NotebookLM.
  • Audio Overviews. Turn your source library into a podcast-style recap. For most personal accounts in 2026, Audio Overviews are available on the free tier. Google can adjust generation limits, but you do not need a paid plan just to try the feature.
  • Sharing. Share notebooks with collaborators when you want a study group or team to work from the same source set.

Free accounts do have limits. Each notebook supports roughly 50 sources, and individual file uploads cap out around 200 MB. Those ceilings are generous for students, solo researchers, and small projects. If you hit them, our source limits guide explains workarounds like splitting topics across notebooks.

NotebookLM Plus and Google One upgrades

Google offers NotebookLM Plus as a paid upgrade. It is typically included with Google One AI Premium and some Google Workspace business plans. The upgrade generally provides higher limits on notebooks and sources per notebook, and Google may roll out Plus-only capabilities over time.

We want to be honest here: Google's packaging changes. Features that start as Plus-only sometimes move to the free tier, and limits get revised without much warning. Before you subscribe, check Google's current pricing page for the latest NotebookLM Plus benefits and monthly cost in your region. If you already pay for Google One AI Premium for Gemini Advanced or extra storage, you may already have Plus access without an extra line item.

Who actually needs the upgrade? Heavy power users who manage dozens of large projects simultaneously, teams that bump against notebook quotas, or professionals who want the highest published limits. Students, writers, and casual researchers usually stay comfortable on the free tier for an entire semester or longer.

How NotebookLM pricing compares to paid AI tools

NotebookLM sits in an unusual spot: it is a specialized research tool that happens to be free, while many general-purpose AI assistants charge monthly fees for their best models.

ChatGPT Plus runs about $20 per month and unlocks OpenAI's most capable models, faster responses, and higher usage caps. It excels at open-ended writing, coding, and brainstorming, but it does not natively ground answers in a curated source library the way NotebookLM does. See our NotebookLM vs ChatGPT comparison for a deeper look.

Claude Pro and similar subscriptions follow the same pattern: pay for smarter models and more messages, not for a document-first workflow with built-in citations. Perplexity Pro adds web search and report generation at a monthly cost. Notion AI, Copilot, and other workspace add-ons charge per seat on top of software you may already pay for.

NotebookLM's free tier delivers something those paid tools do not replicate out of the box: upload your own materials, get cited answers, and generate Audio Overviews from that library. If your workflow centers on studying, researching, or synthesizing documents you already have, NotebookLM's cost advantage is significant. You might still pay for a general AI assistant alongside it, but you are not forced to subscribe just to use NotebookLM well. For how it fits next to Google's other AI products, read NotebookLM vs Gemini.

NotebookTools is free too

Getting sources into NotebookLM is the main friction point for many users. Copying YouTube URLs, switching tabs, and re-uploading the same article eats time across a full course load or research sprint.

NotebookTools is a free Chrome extension that adds a NotebookLM button on YouTube watch pages, web articles, and X posts. Pick a notebook, and the source appears instantly. Highlight text on any page and send just the selection. There is no trial period, no premium tier, and no separate account. It works with your existing Google sign-in. Install from the Chrome Web Store and keep NotebookLM open in another tab for the fastest workflow.

How to get started for free

Go to notebooklm.google.com, sign in with any personal Google account, and click to create your first notebook. There is no checkout step and no trial countdown. Upload a PDF or paste a YouTube URL, ask a question in chat, and you are using the same core product that paid users build on top of. If you are new to the tool, our guide on what NotebookLM is walks through the interface and typical workflows step by step.

When the free tier is enough (and when it is not)

Stay on the free plan if you are a student building one notebook per class, a freelancer researching a single client project, or anyone who works through sources methodically rather than hoarding hundreds per notebook. The combination of free NotebookLM plus free NotebookTools covers lecture videos, PDFs, articles, and exam prep without a subscription bill.

Consider upgrading if you consistently hit source or notebook limits, need team-level Workspace features, or want every Google AI product on the highest tier through one subscription. Even then, try organizing across multiple free notebooks first. Many users discover they never needed Plus once they curate sources instead of dumping everything into one place.

Quick cost summary

  • NotebookLM (personal): Free with a Google account
  • NotebookLM Plus: Paid, usually via Google One AI Premium or Workspace. Check Google's current plans for pricing.
  • NotebookTools: Free Chrome extension
  • ChatGPT Plus / Claude Pro / similar: Separate monthly subscriptions for general AI, not required for NotebookLM

FAQ

Is NotebookLM free to use?
Yes. Sign in with a Google account and you get notebooks, sources, chat, and Audio Overviews on the free tier.

What is NotebookLM Plus?
A paid upgrade with higher limits and possible extra features, bundled with Google One AI Premium or some Workspace plans. Benefits evolve, so verify on Google's pricing page before buying.

Do I need a paid plan for Audio Overviews?
No. Most personal users can generate Audio Overviews for free. Google may adjust limits, but Plus is not required to try the feature.

Is NotebookTools free?
Yes. All import features are free with no upgrade tier. See what NotebookTools is for details.
