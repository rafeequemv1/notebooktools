NotebookLM pricing plans explained (2026)

June 24, 2026 · 9 min read

NotebookLM pricing is simpler than most AI tools: the core product is free with a Google account. Paid plans exist, but they are optional upgrades for people who need higher limits or business features. If you searched notebooklm pricing or notebooklm plans, this guide maps every tier in plain English.

Google does change packaging over time, so treat exact dollar amounts as pointers, not promises. Always check Google One plans or your Workspace admin for current numbers. For a yes/no on the free tier, see is NotebookLM free. For product basics, start with what NotebookLM is.

Free plan: what most people use

The free personal tier is the default for anyone with a Google account. You sign in at notebooklm.google.com and start building notebooks immediately. No credit card, no trial countdown.

Typical free limits in 2026 include roughly 50 sources per notebook and file upload caps around 200 MB each. That is enough for a full semester course, a client project, or a personal research sprint. Details and workarounds are in our source limits guide.

Free users also get Audio Overviews, notebook sharing, and the full chat experience grounded in uploaded sources. If you are comparing NotebookLM to $20/month AI subscriptions, the free tier is the headline feature.

NotebookLM Plus: when an upgrade makes sense

NotebookLM Plus is Google's paid upgrade path. It is not sold as a standalone NotebookLM subscription in most regions. Instead, it ships inside Google One AI Premium, which also bundles Gemini Advanced, extra cloud storage, and other Google AI perks.

Plus generally raises limits: more notebooks, more sources per notebook, and sometimes faster or priority access to new features. Google may also test Plus-only capabilities before rolling them wider. The exact list changes, which is why we link to Google's plans page instead of quoting a fixed feature table that goes stale.

Upgrade if you consistently hit free quotas across many active projects, or if you already pay for Google One AI Premium and want to use every included benefit. Skip it if one or two notebooks cover your current work. Our 2026 review covers whether the free tier is enough for most readers.

Workspace and enterprise pricing

Businesses using Google Workspace may get NotebookLM through admin-enabled plans. Pricing is per user per month on Business Starter, Standard, Plus, or Enterprise SKUs. Admins control who can access NotebookLM, how data is handled, and whether Plus-level limits apply.

Enterprise buyers care about compliance, sharing policies, and support channels more than individual students do. If you are a solo user, Workspace pricing is irrelevant. If your company already runs on Google, ask your IT admin whether NotebookLM is enabled on your plan.

How NotebookLM cost compares to other AI tools

NotebookLM is unusual because the research workflow (upload sources, get cited answers) is free. General assistants charge monthly fees for premium models:

  • ChatGPT Plus (~$20/month): broad chat, coding, writing. Less source-grounded than NotebookLM. Comparison
  • Claude Pro (~$20/month): strong reasoning and writing, no native notebook model.
  • Gemini Advanced (via Google One AI Premium): overlaps with Plus bundle. Comparison
  • Perplexity Pro: web search and reports, different workflow.

Many people use NotebookLM free for document work and keep one paid assistant for everything else. That combo often costs less than stacking multiple research subscriptions.

Which plan should you pick?

Pick free if you are a student, solo researcher, or writer testing NotebookLM for the first time. Follow our beginner tutorial and stay on free until you hit real limits.

Pick Plus if you already want Google One AI Premium for Gemini and storage, or if you manage many large notebooks and need the highest published quotas.

Pick Workspace only through your organization. It is a business decision, not a personal subscription choice.

FAQ

How much does NotebookLM cost?
Free for personal use. Paid access comes through NotebookLM Plus (Google One AI Premium) or Workspace business plans.

What is NotebookLM Plus?
A paid upgrade with higher limits, usually bundled with Google One AI Premium.

Do I need Google One for NotebookLM?
No. A free Google account is enough for the standard experience.

Is there a student discount?
Most students use the free tier. No separate student plan is required for typical coursework.
